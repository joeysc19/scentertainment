Vol. IV No. 22 Saturday, May 23, 2026 Price: Free Exchange

Feature Review

The Twilight Symphony Returns to the Hinterland Range

By Alistair Vance • Cultural Correspondent

Under the canopy of the ancient Maleny rainforest, classical strings met open-air acoustics this weekend. The annual Hinterland Symphony showcase gathered over four hundred patrons on the grass slopes, proving once again that the Coast’s appetite for high art extends far beyond its famous shorelines.

The standout performance belonged to the local youth conservatory ensemble, who tackled Vivaldi's compositions with a fierce, modern tempo. As twilight faded behind the Glass House Mountains silhouette, the production escalated into an immersive light display mapped directly across the timber staging, leaving the crowd completely spellbound.
